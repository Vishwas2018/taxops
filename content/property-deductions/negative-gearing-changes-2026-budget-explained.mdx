The 2026-27 Budget announced a change to how negative gearing works for residential property.
The reform has since been legislated (the Treasury Laws Amendment (Tax Reform No. 1) Act 2026
received Royal Assent on 26 June 2026), but its substantive changes are **not yet in effect** -
they don't apply to any return until the 2027-28 income year. Every return covering the current
and next financial year is unaffected, and existing negative gearing rules continue to apply in
the meantime exactly as they do today.

## What's changing, from 1 July 2027

From 1 July 2027, losses on an established (not newly built) residential investment property
purchased from 7:30pm AEST on 12 May 2026 will only be deductible against other income from
residential properties, including capital gains on those properties - not against salary, wages,
or other income the way a rental loss can be used today. Where losses exceed that property
income in a given year, the excess can be carried forward to offset residential property income
in future years, rather than being lost.

## Who's grandfathered

Every property held before 7:30pm AEST on 12 May 2026 - including where a contract had been
entered into but not yet settled - keeps access to negative gearing under the current rules for
as long as that property is held. Nothing changes for an existing investor's existing holdings;
the reform only affects the tax treatment of losses on established properties **acquired** from
the announcement time onward.

## The transitional window: purchased after announcement, before 1 July 2027

A property purchased between the announcement (7:30pm AEST 12 May 2026) and 30 June 2027 can
still be negatively geared during that window - the new treatment only starts from 1 July 2027
onward, regardless of when the property was purchased within that window.

## New builds are treated differently

A newly constructed residential property that genuinely adds to housing supply - built on
vacant land, or a knock-down rebuild that increases the number of dwellings - keeps access to
negative gearing (and the current 50% CGT discount) both before and after 1 July 2027, for as
long as it hasn't already been sold to a subsequent owner. A knock-down rebuild that doesn't
increase the number of dwellings, a substantial renovation, or a granny flat added to an
established property does not qualify as a new build under this test.

## What this doesn't cover

This describes the negative gearing change only - the related capital gains tax discount reform
(replacing the 50% discount with cost base indexation and a minimum tax rate) is covered
separately. It doesn't cover commercial property (unaffected), how the changes interact with a
specific ownership structure (individual, trust, company, or partnership), or how carried-forward
losses are tracked and applied in practice - those depend on the specific property and investor
circumstances involved.

## Questions for your registered tax agent

- Does a specific property purchase (or contract date) fall before or after the 7:30pm AEST 12
  May 2026 announcement time?
- Does a planned purchase meet the new build definition, and does that classification survive a
  later sale to a subsequent owner?
- How would carried-forward residential property losses interact with an existing portfolio that
  includes both pre- and post-announcement properties?
- What ownership structure (individual, trust, company) is being used, and how does the reform's
  scope apply to it specifically?
